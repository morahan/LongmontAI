## The previous two weeks of AI model releases

The two weeks leading into June 10 were unusually dense on the release calendar. Walking back from the meetup date:

- **June 9 — Anthropic Claude Fable 5.** The new flagship. SWE-Bench Pro ~80%, HLE 53%, AA Intelligence Index 65 (the top score on the board). 1M context, $10 in / $50 out per million tokens. System-card numbers are still pending; the 65 is an early third-party reading.
- **Early June — MiniMax M3.** Dropped just before the meetup as a top-tier agentic coding entrant. MiniMax's hosted M2.5 stays on as a strong free option for builders who want frontier-ish quality without a credit card.
- **Late May — Kimi K2.6 (Moonshot AI).** Modified-MIT open weights. Currently leads LiveBench coding among open-weight models and ships a "thinking" variant built for sub-agent parallelism — a meaningful unlock for anyone orchestrating fleets of coding agents.
- **Late May — DeepSeek V4.** 1.6T params (49B active) under MIT, 1M context, with both a Pro and a 284B Flash. The API priced from $0.44 / $0.87 per million tokens — the "pricing earthquake" tier that keeps dragging the open-weights floor closer to free.
- **Late May — GLM-5.1 (Z.ai / Zhipu).** MIT-licensed, SOTA on SWE-Bench Pro among open models, with sustained agentic iteration across thousands of tool calls. Quietly one of the most usable long-horizon agent models you can self-host.
- **Late May — Qwen 3.6 (Alibaba).** Apache 2.0, with two practical local picks: the 27B dense and the 35B-A3B MoE. The 35B-A3B posts 49.5 SWE-Bench Pro and 51.5 Terminal-Bench, and runs on a DGX Spark.
- **Late May — Gemma 4 (Google, open weights).** The 31B is the new on-device / edge champion, sitting alongside Mistral Small 4 as the best small-hardware picks of the cycle.
- **Earlier in Q2 — Claude Opus 4.8** (Anthropic) became the daily-driver value pick for agentic coding at $5 / $25.
- **April — GPT-5.5 (OpenAI)** with SWE-Bench Verified 88.7%, and **Grok 4.3 (xAI)** at the cheapest frontier price point ($1.25 / $2.50) with live X / web data.
- **February — Gemini 3.1 Pro (Google)** with a 2M context window, GPQA Diamond 94.3%, and the ARC-AGI-2 lead.

Two weeks, eight meaningful releases, and a non-trivial number of them are open weights. The closed-frontier / open-frontier gap for *coding* in particular has nearly closed — that is the single most important sentence in the deck.

## What the deck is actually saying

Reading across the four slides:

- **Frontier is now a routing problem, not a picking problem.** Fable 5 wins on raw intelligence, Opus 4.8 wins on price-per-agentic-coding-token, GPT-5.5 wins on SWE-Bench Verified, Gemini 3.1 Pro wins on long-context reasoning, Grok 4.3 wins on live data. The right answer in production is "use the cheapest model that passes your eval on this task," not "always call the leaderboard winner."
- **The harness matters as much as the model.** Slide 2 makes this concrete: Terminal-Bench between the top coding harnesses spans ~15 points on the *same model*. Claude Code is the most feature-complete. Codex CLI is the most open (Apache 2.0, Rust). Gemini CLI is the cheapest managed option. Droid is the team-workflow pick. Pi is the bet that the model *is* the product. Nine serious harnesses now — the wrapper is no longer a footnote.
- **Self-hosted agents are real and risky.** OpenClaw (formerly Moltbot / Steinberger) has crossed 361K GitHub stars and 5,700+ community skills — but it has shipped **3 CVEs and 135K exposed instances** in 2026. Hermes Agent (Nous Research) takes the opposite bet: a seven-layer security model, a self-improving skill loop, Python-native. NanoClaw ships in 3.9K lines of code and runs happily on a $3.50/mo VPS. The category is real. The security model is not solved.
- **Open weights caught up on coding.** Kimi K2.6 leads LiveBench coding. GLM-5.1 leads SWE-Bench Pro among open models. DeepSeek V4 reset the price floor. Qwen 3.6 and Gemma 4 cover local-from-laptop to on-device. If your bottleneck is "can I run a frontier-class coding model locally?" the answer is now yes.

## What to do with this on Monday

For Longmont builders, the practical read-out is short:

- Stop picking one frontier model. Route by task and by token cost.
- Pick your coding harness on the basis of the team and the security posture, not the leaderboard.
- If you're self-hosting agents, treat the security model as a first-class requirement — the 135K exposed OpenClaw instances are the lesson.
- The open-weights coding frontier is real enough to build a product on. If you've been waiting for a reason to self-host, this is the cycle.

Frontier moved. Harness matters. Agents are real and dangerous. Open weights caught up. That is the AI landscape on June 10, 2026 — one day after Claude Fable 5.
