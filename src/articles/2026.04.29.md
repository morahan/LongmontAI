## The Physical Reality Behind GPT 5.5

Last Wednesday's meetup used GPT 5.5 as the jumping-off point for a bigger question: what has to be true outside the chat window for frontier AI to keep scaling? The deck follows the physical reality behind the software leap, from data quality and compute supply to power, water, infrastructure, talent, and public trust.

{{slideshow:scaling-ai}}

**The Physical Reality of AI: 2026 State of the Union** argues that AI capability is no longer just a benchmark curve. It tracks rapid software adoption, scientific and clinical workflow replacement, the synthetic-data ceiling, global compute growth, Nvidia-centered supply-chain concentration, power-grid pressure, data-center water use, and the carbon cost of high-volume inference.

Its final message is that brute-force scaling is running into a trilemma: high-quality data, gigawatt power, and silicon fabrication are all scarce at once. The strategic answer is to pivot toward data quality, predictive infrastructure, and sovereign capability rather than assuming bigger models and more scraped data will carry the next phase alone.
