Logo

Clear space & minimum size

  • Maintain clear space equal to the cap height of "L" on every side of the lockup.
  • Minimum print size: 32mm wide for the horizontal lockup.
  • Minimum digital size: 140px wide. Below that, switch to the parrot mark or favicon.

Do / don't

DOPlace the wordmark on a solid brand surface with sufficient contrast.
DON'TPlace the color logo on busy photography or low-contrast backgrounds.
DOUse the parrot icon alone when the wordmark won't fit (avatars, app icons, watermarks).
DON'TRecolor, restretch, rotate, or add effects (drop shadow, stroke, glow) to the mark.
DOTreat the parrot as the letter "I" — it sits at cap height, baseline-aligned with the text.
DON'TSubstitute the parrot for any letter other than "I", or float it disconnected from the wordmark.

Two cyans — pick deliberately

  • Cyan #00F0FF — for content energy. Hero eyebrow ("AI-GENERATED ART"), sparkles icon, gradient text. Reads as "AI / future."
  • Blue #007CF0 — for UI chrome. Nav links, the Countdown pill border, info banners. Reads as "trustworthy link."

Usage rules

  • Sunset is the warm highlight — eyebrows, hero accents. Don't use for body type.
  • Cyan signals editorial energy. Reserve for moments you want to feel "AI."
  • Blue is the everyday link color. Use it for any interactive surface.
  • Purple powers the signature gradient and the card hover glow. Don't paint solid blocks of it.
  • Pink is the closing gradient stop and callout color. Don't use for body type.
  • Surfaces are Zinc 950 → 900 → 800. Always test card-on-bg contrast before shipping.

Typography

Display and body type both use Beleza, a warm contemporary serif that pairs naturally with the cubist mark. When Beleza is unavailable, fall back to Charter (macOS), Georgia, or any serif system font. Editorial metadata uses JetBrains Mono.

The hero artwork has a 5-second motion variant. Use it sparingly — once per page, ideally above the fold. Always provide a poster image so the visual loads instantly.

Voice

Curious, generous, humble. We are a community — never marketers. Lead with what attendees will learn and build, not what they will buy. Keep tone plainspoken and welcoming. Assume a smart audience that ranges from "AI-curious" to "ships models for a living."
